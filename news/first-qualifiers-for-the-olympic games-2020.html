First qualifiers for the Olympic games 2020

Home News First qualifiers for the Olympic games 2020
18 August, 2019 Jelena Žegarac 3 1
  • olympics
  • competition

Having won the 2018 IFSC Combined World Championship and swept the 2019 IFSC World Cup bouldering season, Slovenia’s Janja Garnbret is a favorite leading up to the 2020 Tokyo Olympics.

Some of the greatest Sport Climbing athletes have already secured their ticket for the Olympic Games in Tokyo 2020! However, a living legend, Adam Ondra (CZE) is still uncertain.

Climbing World Championship, recently held in Hachioji, Japan, has brought many nervous moments for climbers. After competing in Combined format (one day mix of Lead, Boulder and Speed), climbing community got their first participants for the Olympics next year. According to the rules, only 2 athletes per country may qualify (plus an extra one for a host). Thus, the Japanese team was quite under pressure as they had 4 female and 4 male representatives competing in the finals.

After so many back-to-back exhausting days for competitors, IFSC announced the first group of qualified athletes in the history of Sport Climbing.

Female

  1. Janja Garnbret (SLO)
  2. Akiyo Noguchi (JPN)
  3. Shauna Coxsey (GBR)
  4. Aleksandra Miroslaw (POL)
  5. Miho Nonaka (JPN)
  6. Petra Klinger (SUI)
  7. Brooke Raboutou (USA)

Male

  1. Tomoa Narasaki (JPN)
  2. Jakob Schubert (AUT)
  3. Rishat Khaibullin (KAZ)
  4. Kai Harada (JPN)
  5. Mickael Mawem (FRA)
  6. Alexander Megos (GER)
  7. Ludovico Fossali (ITA)

Other athletes will have the opportunity to qualify for Tokyo 2020 in the next event in late November in Tournefeuille-Toulouse, France. Six additional athletes will get the clear passage to the Olympics after this event.

With the competition scene entering the big stage for the 2020 Olympics, Ondra has decided to give everything to indoor climbing in hopes of taking home the gold. The first big test? Next month’s IFSC World Championships in Innsbruck, Austria. This video documents Ondra’s recent training on the long road to Tokyo.
